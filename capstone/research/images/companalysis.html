Site 1: Design + Art History by Webflow

The first site that I found that I thought emulated the same user experience I want for my project is one designed by Webflow. When you first visit the website, you'll notice the sleek and modern design that instantly draws you in. The color scheme, which mainly consists of shades of grey with hints of vibrant red, is both striking and visually pleasing, making you want to explore further. The website's layout is thoughtfully organized, making it easy to find your way around and locate the information you need. As you interact with the site, you'll appreciate the smooth and intuitive interaction design, with subtle hover effects providing a delightful user experience. Plus, the quizzes and animations add a fun and engaging touch to the learning experience, making it all the more enjoyable. Overall, the website offers a truly top-notch user experience that balances design and education beautifully.

Site 2: PORSCHEvolution

The second site is an incredible history dive into the evolution or Porche, or PORCHEvolution. The Porschevolution site has a sleek and modern interface design, with a black and white color scheme and bold typography that makes it easy to navigate. The interaction design is engaging, as users can click on each decade in the timeline to reveal more information about Porsche's history during that time period. The site also features high-quality images and videos that showcase Porsche's iconic cars, adding to the overall user experience. However, the site could benefit from clearer navigation cues, as some users may find it difficult to understand how to access all the available content. Additionally, the lack of mobile responsiveness may limit the site's accessibility for users browsing on smaller devices.
